# Erdős #251 "S1 carrier": a verdicted per-candidate checklist

## (1) Headline verdict

**NOT-FOUND at literature grain.** After searching the word-grain/tuple mean-value, short-interval-moment, singular-series-with-growing-k, Maier-matrix, entropy, multiplicative-correlation, and symbolic-dynamics axes, no published or preprint theorem matches the governing sentence: an *unconditional*, *averaged*, *growing-rank* (k ~ 2.885 log log x), *word-grain* non-concentration / upper-mean-value statement for middle gaps of matched flank words inside a window h ≍ (log x)(log log x) — even under the fully relaxed demand triple (constant-order strength, no s-uniformity, sparse/liminf scale). The nearest unconditional results each fall short on a single named axis, and the one methodological template that recently went unconditional for a structurally similar problem (Tao–Teräväinen for Σ ω(n)/2ⁿ, arXiv:2512.01739, which states "we show that the series Σ_{n=1}^∞ ω(n)/2^n is irrational, settling a conjecture of Erdős … we … make use of a general quantitative estimate for two-point correlations of multiplicative functions with a small power of logarithm saving … derived by using recent work of Pilatte") does so only for *multiplicative* observables and does not reach the prime-indicator/gap-word grain. Every conditional sighting rests on a uniform Hardy–Littlewood prime k-tuples hypothesis. This is a located absence relative to the searched inventory, not an impossibility claim.

### Candidate A — Kuperberg, "Sums of singular series with large sets and the tail of the distribution of primes"
- **Citation.** V. Kuperberg, *Q. J. Math.* 74 (2023) 1457–1479, DOI 10.1093/qmath/haad030; arXiv:2210.09775v2 [math.NT], 15 Jun 2023. Accessed 24 Jul 2026.
- **Governing sentences (verbatim).** Theorem 1.1: "Fix δ > 1/2, and let h, k ∈ ℕ with k = O((log h)^{1−δ}). Let T_k(h) be given by [T_k(h) := Σ_{h₁,…,h_k ≤ h distinct} 𝔖(h₁,…,h_k)]. Then there exists a β > 0, dependent only on δ > 1/2, with T_k(h) = h^k + O(h^{k−β})." The prime application (abstract): "assuming appropriate Hardy–Littlewood conjectures and in certain ranges of the parameters, the number of intervals [n, n+λ log x] with n ≤ x containing at least k primes is ≪ x exp(−k/(λe))." That application invokes Conjecture 1.3: "There exist two absolute constants ε > 0 and C > 0 such that for all x, for all k ≤ (log log x)³, and for all admissible tuples H = {h₁,…,h_k} ⊂ [0,(log x)²], |Σ_{n≤x} 1_P(n+h₁)···1_P(n+h_k) − 𝔖(H)li_k(x)| ≤ Cx^{1−ε}."
- **Verdict.** NEAR-MISS on three axes simultaneously. (i) *Rank uniformity*: the unconditional Theorem 1.1 admits only k = O((log h)^{1/2−ε}). With the target window h ≍ (log x)(log log x), log h ≍ log log x, so the unconditional range is k ≲ (log log x)^{1/2}, whereas the carrier needs k ~ 2.885 log log x — a quadratic shortfall (the author explicitly conjectures the average persists to k = O((log h)²), which would cover the target, but that is unproved). (ii) *Grain*: Theorem 1.1 controls the *singular-series average* (a model quantity), not the empirical word-grain masses N_{P,d} of actual primes. (iii) *Unconditional vs conditional*: every statement about actual prime counts (the tail bound ≪ x exp(−k/(λe))) is conditional on the uniform Hardy–Littlewood Conjecture 1.3.
- **Both-readings.** *Supports*: singular-series averages are provably tractable to a growing rank k ≲ (log h)^{1/2}, giving a concrete unconditional beachhead on the growing-rank axis. *Contradicts*: precisely at the target rank (k a fixed multiple of log log x, i.e. up to order (log h)²) only a *conjecture*, not a theorem, is available; and the controlled object is the model singular series, not the prime realisations.

### Candidate B — de la Bretèche–Fiorilli, "On a conjecture of Montgomery and Soundararajan"
- **Citation.** R. de la Bretèche, D. Fiorilli, *Math. Ann.* 381 (2021) 575–591, DOI 10.1007/s00208-021-02179-6; arXiv:2009.05760v1, 12 Sep 2020. Accessed 24 Jul 2026.
- **Governing sentence (verbatim, abstract).** "We establish lower bounds for all weighted even moments of primes up to X in intervals which are in agreement with a conjecture of Montgomery and Soundararajan. Our bounds hold unconditionally for an unbounded set of values of X, and hold for all X under the Riemann Hypothesis. We also deduce new unconditional Ω-results for the classical prime counting function."
- **Verdict.** NEAR-MISS. This is the strongest match on the **sparse-scale/liminf axis**: "unconditionally for an unbounded set of values of X" is exactly the relaxed scale-density class the search key foregrounds. But it misses on four other axes: (i) it is a **lower** bound (an Ω-result / dispersion-from-below), whereas the carrier needs an **upper** non-concentration bound Q ≤ (1−η)N; (ii) **fixed** moment order n, no growing rank k; (iii) **count-grain** (ψ(x+h)−ψ(x)), not the word-grain masses N_{P,d}; (iv) window δX with (log X)^{1+ε}/X ≤ δ ≤ X^{−ε}, i.e. **polynomially large** h = δX, not h ≍ (log x)(log log x).
- **Both-readings.** *Supports*: proves that a genuinely arithmetic short-interval second-moment statement can hold *unconditionally along an unbounded scale sequence* — the precise structural relaxation the project relies on. *Contradicts*: the delivered inequality is a lower bound at fixed moment order in a polynomial window, none of which is the upper, growing-rank, logarithmic-window word-grain quantity required.

### Candidate C — Montgomery–Soundararajan, "Primes in short intervals"
- **Citation.** H. L. Montgomery, K. Soundararajan, *Comm. Math. Phys.* 252 (2004) 589–617, DOI 10.1007/s00220-004-1222-4; arXiv:math/0409258. Accessed 24 Jul 2026.
- **Governing statement (verbatim, via T. H. Chan's transcription math/0409530 and the de la Bretèche–Fiorilli restatement).** The moment asymptotic M_k(N;h) = μ_k h^{k/2}∫₁^N(log(x/h)+B)^{k/2}dx + O_k(h^{k/2}N^{1−ε}) holds "under a strong form of Hardy-Littlewood prime-k tuple conjecture, … uniformly for (log N)^{15k²} ≤ h ≤ N^{1/k−δ}." Conjecture 1.1 (as restated by dlB–F): "Fix ε > 0. For each fixed n ∈ ℕ and uniformly for (log X)^{1+ε}/X ≤ δ ≤ 1/X^ε, [the n-th moment is (μ_n+o(1))(δ log δ^{−1})^{n/2}]."
- **Verdict.** CONDITIONAL-ONLY. Exact hypothesis: "a strong form of the Hardy–Littlewood prime-k tuple conjecture." The result is also count-grain and lives in a polynomially large window (log N)^{15k²} ≤ h, squarely outside the logarithmic-scale target window.
- **Both-readings.** *Supports*: it is the canonical model for what a full Gaussian/moment statement for primes in short intervals would look like, and fixes the shape of the object the carrier weakens. *Contradicts*: conditional on prime k-tuples, wrong window, wrong grain.

### Candidate D — Maier, "Primes in short intervals" (the re-priced row; full sub-task in §3)
- **Citation.** H. Maier, *Michigan Math. J.* 32 (1985) 221–225, DOI 10.1307/mmj/1029003189, MR 0783576, Zbl 0569.10023. Accessed 24 Jul 2026.
- **Governing statement (verbatim, as transcribed in the literature).** For Φ(x) := (log x)^λ, λ > 1: "limsup_{x→∞} [π(x+Φ(x))−π(x)]/[Φ(x)/log x] > 1 and liminf_{x→∞} [π(x+Φ(x))−π(x)]/[Φ(x)/log x] < 1." Equivalently (Wikipedia's faithful paraphrase): the ratio "does not have a limit as x tends to infinity; more precisely the limit superior is greater than 1, and the limit inferior is less than 1."
- **Verdict.** NEAR-MISS. Unconditional, and it is an *oscillation along a special sequence of x* — hence in the admissible sparse-scale class. It **passes the model-failure filter** (it contradicts the Cramér model, which predicts limit 1 for λ ≥ 2). But it is **count-grain** (prime counts π(x+Φ)−π(x)), has **no growing tuple dimension**, and its window Φ(x) = (log x)^λ with λ **fixed** is a genuinely shorter/different object from h ≍ (log x)(log log x) carrying a growing word of gaps.
- **Both-readings.** *Supports*: demonstrates, unconditionally and along a special scale sequence, a genuinely arithmetic short-interval irregularity that a smooth deterministic model cannot reproduce — the exact logical template the relaxed demand triple wants. *Contradicts*: it controls a scalar prime count, not the multiplicity structure N_{P,d} of a growing-length gap word, and does not produce a positive-proportion coincidence of matched flank words with distinct middles.

### Candidate E — Tao–Teräväinen, "Quantitative correlations and some problems on prime factors of consecutive integers"
- **Citation.** T. Tao, J. Teräväinen, arXiv:2512.01739v2 [math.NT], 25 Apr 2026 (60 pp.). Accessed 24 Jul 2026.
- **Governing sentences (verbatim).** Theorem 1.3: "The series Σ_{n=1}^∞ ω(n)/2ⁿ = Σ_p 1/(2^p−1) = 0.5169428… is irrational." Engine, Theorem 3.1: "Suppose that g₁, g₂ : ℕ → ℂ are 1-bounded multiplicative functions. … Then, there exists a set 𝓔 ⊂ [√X, X] satisfying the logarithmic density bound (1/log X)∫_𝓔 dt/t ≪ 𝓛^{−c} such that for any W ∈ [𝓛^c] and integers b, h₁, h₂ = O(𝓛^c) with h₁ ≠ h₂, we have (W/N) Σ_{N<n≤2N} (g₁(n+h₁) − δ_N) g₂(n+h₂) 1_{n≡b (mod W)} ≪ 𝓛^{−c} for all N ∈ [√X, X] \ 𝓔."
- **Verdict.** NOT-A-CARRIER (structural reason). Theorem 3.1 requires the observables to be **1-bounded multiplicative functions**; the prime indicator 1_P is not multiplicative and the von Mangoldt Λ is not 1-bounded, so neither the middle-gap indicator nor the prime-tuple counting weight is admissible input. The paper does not address consecutive prime gaps or gap words at all. (Notably, the estimate does hold on the "sparse-scale" axis the project wants — "for all N ∈ [√X, X] \ 𝓔" with 𝓔 of small logarithmic density, i.e. almost-all scales — confirming the sparse-scale regime is non-vacuous, but for the wrong observable class.)
- **Both-readings.** *Supports*: proof-of-concept that a growing-rank sieve (Maynard-type of dimension ⌊(1/C₀)log₂x⌋) combined with almost-all-scales correlation estimates can settle an Erdős-#251-adjacent irrationality problem unconditionally. *Contradicts*: the whole apparatus is confined to multiplicative observables (ω, Ω, τ); the authors flag that even the ω-"barrier" refinements hit prime-tuples/Sophie-Germain difficulty at small shifts (Remark 1.2: "to handle small values of k, such as k = 1, 2, it seems that the problem is of comparable difficulty to the prime tuples conjecture (or the Sophie Germain conjecture …), and we make no progress on it here"); nothing here reaches the prime-gap-word grain. The paper contains **no explicit "parity problem" limitation statement** — the exclusion of primes is enforced only implicitly through Theorem 3.1's multiplicativity hypothesis.

### Candidate F — Pilatte, "Improved bounds for the two-point logarithmic Chowla conjecture"
- **Citation.** C. Pilatte, arXiv:2310.19357v2 [math.NT], 1 Dec 2025. Accessed 24 Jul 2026.
- **Governing sentence (verbatim, abstract).** "We prove that Σ_{n≤x} (1/n) λ(n) λ(n+1) ≪ (log x)^{1−c} for some absolute constant c > 0. This appears to be best possible with current methods."
- **Verdict.** NOT-A-CARRIER. Unconditional and at almost all scales, but the observable is the Liouville function λ (completely multiplicative, ±1-valued). It provides the log-power saving that Tao–Teräväinen's Theorem 3.1 imports, but it never touches the prime-indicator or gap grain.
- **Both-readings.** *Supports*: shows an unconditional, log-power-saving two-point correlation bound is attainable for a genuinely arithmetic 1-bounded function. *Contradicts*: multiplicative/parity-controlled observable only; no growing rank; no gap words.

### Candidate G — Pratt, "The irrationality of a prime factor series under a prime tuples conjecture"
- **Citation.** K. Pratt, arXiv:2409.15185v1 [math.NT], 23 Sep 2024; published *J. Number Theory* (2025), S0022314X25001209. Accessed 24 Jul 2026.
- **Governing sentence (verbatim, abstract).** "Assuming a suitably uniform version of the prime k-tuples conjecture, we show that the number Σ_{n=1}^∞ ω(n)/2ⁿ is irrational."
- **Verdict.** CONDITIONAL-ONLY. Exact hypothesis: "a suitably uniform version of the prime k-tuples conjecture" (his Conjecture 1.1 with quantitative uniformity in K). This is the conditional predecessor to Candidate E for the same sum, and the closest published instance of the "stronger demand" the search key says previously excluded the sparse-scale classes a priori.
- **Both-readings.** *Supports*: pinpoints exactly which uniform hypothesis suffices, and (read against Candidate E) shows that hypothesis can be *removed* for the multiplicative observable ω. *Contradicts*: conditional; multiplicative grain.

### Candidate H — Gallagher, "On the distribution of primes in short intervals"
- **Citation.** P. X. Gallagher, *Mathematika* 23 (1976) 4–9, Cambridge Core S0025579300016442. Accessed 24 Jul 2026.
- **Governing content (verbatim).** "assuming a certain uniform version of the (unproved) prime r-tuple conjecture of Hardy and Littlewood … the distribution tends to the Poisson distribution with parameter λ as N→∞. Using a sieve upper bound for the r-tuple problem, we also get an unconditional exponential upper bound for the tail of the distribution."
- **Verdict.** NOT-A-CARRIER. k (= r) is fixed throughout; the Poisson theorem is conditional on Hardy–Littlewood; count-grain. (The *unconditional* tail bound Gallagher does prove via a sieve upper bound is at fixed r and about counts, not word-grain multiplicities.)
- **Both-readings.** *Supports*: origin of the log-window Poisson heuristic the carrier's model rests on, and shows a sieve upper bound gives *some* unconditional tail control at fixed r. *Contradicts*: fixed k, conditional Poisson, count-grain.

## (3) The Maier re-pricing sub-task
- **(i) What the method actually delivers.** Maier's 1985 theorem, proved by the matrix method (rows = translates of an interval by multiples of a primorial; columns analysed via Gallagher's 1970 large-sieve density estimate for primes in progressions), shows that for **fixed** λ > 1 the normalised prime **count** [π(x+(log x)^λ)−π(x)] / ((log x)^λ/log x) has limsup > 1 and liminf < 1 — i.e. the count oscillates and has no limit, **along special sequences of x** produced by the construction. Native output: oscillation of a scalar prime count in a window (log x)^λ, λ fixed, along a sparse special scale sequence.
- **(ii) Can any descendant reach a positive-proportion growing-length gap-word statement?** Not in the located literature. The matrix method and its descendants (Maier–Pomerance large gaps; Maier–Stewart; Granville–Lumley heuristics 2009.05000; Friedlander–Granville–Hildebrand–Maier progression irregularities; Pintz 2007 "Cramér vs. Cramér") all output statements about prime **counts / gap sizes** in intervals, obtained by exhibiting one favourable row. None produces control of the **multiplicity masses** N_{P,d} of a growing-length (~ log log x) consecutive-gap **word** with distinct middles, nor a positive-proportion (Q ≤ (1−η)N) coincidence statement. The method's native output is "at least one interval/row has property Z" — an existence / lower-density statement, not an upper mean-value over word classes.
- **(iii) Model compatibility.** Maier's theorem **fails in the smooth deterministic model**: it directly contradicts the Cramér prediction (limit = 1 for λ ≥ 2), and a smooth monotone gap sequence yields a determinate limit, so it lacks the arithmetic oscillation Maier exhibits. Thus Maier passes the model-failure filter — but on the wrong (count) grain.
- **Support class: PROVED** (Maier's own theorem). **Re-priced verdict: NEAR-MISS**, gap on the axes: *count-grain vs word-grain* (primary), *fixed-λ window vs (log x)(log log x)*, *no growing tuple dimension*.

## (4) The "S2 word-grain substitute" question
Is there an unconditional lower-moment / positive-proportion word-grain substitute for the Montgomery–Soundararajan / Kuperberg line at the required window and growing rank? **No such substitute is located.** The only unconditional lower-moment technology that reaches logarithmically-short windows and almost-all/unbounded scale sequences is the **multiplicative-function correlation line** (Matomäki–Radziwiłł 1501.04585 and II 2007.04290; Pilatte 2310.19357; Tao–Teräväinen 2512.01739). That line is structurally confined to 1-bounded multiplicative (or additive, via ω/Ω) observables and, per Tao–Teräväinen's Theorem 3.1, does not admit the prime indicator 1_P (not multiplicative) or Λ (not 1-bounded) as input; nor does any of these papers treat consecutive-gap words. The de la Bretèche–Fiorilli sparse-scale Ω-results are the closest genuinely-prime substitute but are lower bounds at fixed moment order in a polynomial window. Consequently there is at present **no unconditional word-grain lower-moment or positive-proportion analogue** of MS/Kuperberg at h ≍ (log x)(log log x) and rank k ~ 2.885 log log x.

## (5) Closest known unconditional statements (ranked)
1. **Kuperberg Theorem 1.1 (2210.09775).** Single missing axis: **rank range** — unconditional only to k ≲ (log h)^{1/2−ε}, versus target k of order (log h)² (a quadratic shortfall); also model-quantity, not prime realisations.
2. **de la Bretèche–Fiorilli (2009.05760).** Single missing axis: **direction/grain** — an unconditional *lower* bound (Ω-result), sparse-scale, but count-grain at fixed moment order and polynomial window; not the required upper word-grain bound.
3. **Maier 1985.** Single missing axis: **count-grain vs word-grain** — unconditional, sparse-scale, model-failing oscillation, but of a scalar count in a fixed-λ window with no growing rank.
4. **Tao–Teräväinen (2512.01739) + Pilatte (2310.19357).** Single missing axis: **observable class** — unconditional almost-all-scales correlation control, but only for 1-bounded multiplicative functions, not prime/gap indicators.
5. **Matomäki–Radziwiłł II (2007.04290).** Single missing axis: **observable class / grain** — unconditional power-saving in almost all short intervals of any length, but for multiplicative functions vanishing at a positive proportion of primes, not gap words.
