### 1. Erdős #251 and irrationality analogues

- T. F. Bloom, “Erdős Problem #251,” <https://www.erdosproblems.com/251>, accessed 2026-07-16. This is the repository’s established citation form for the open problem considered here.
- “Forum thread for Erdős Problem #251,” *Erdős Problems*, <https://www.erdosproblems.com/forum/thread/251>, accessed 2026-07-16. An operator browser check verified all four posts, including Tao’s prime-gap reformulation and proposed uniform-prime-tuples route.
- P. Erdős, “Sur certaines séries à valeur irrationnelle,” *L’Enseignement Mathématique* (2) 4 (1958), 93–100. This is the original source identified for Erdős Problem #251 and proves the factorial-denominator case with first-power prime numerators.
- J.-C. Schlage-Puchta, “The irrationality of some number theoretical series,” *Acta Arithmetica* 126 (2007), no. 4, 295–303, [DOI 10.4064/aa126-4-1](https://doi.org/10.4064/aa126-4-1); [arXiv:1105.1451v1 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1105.1451v1). The later arXiv posting and the 2007 journal article are the same work.
- K. Pratt, “The irrationality of a prime factor series under a prime tuples conjecture,” *Journal of Number Theory* 276 (2025), 270–285, [DOI 10.1016/j.jnt.2025.02.010](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jnt.2025.02.010); [arXiv:2409.15185v1 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2409.15185v1). This conditionally proves the irrationality of $\sum_{n\ge1}\omega(n)/2^n$. Its Conjecture 1.2 permits $a_k,b_k\le(\log\log x)^{100}$ and $K\le100\log\log\log x$.
- T. Tao and J. Teräväinen, “Quantitative correlations and some problems on prime factors of consecutive integers,” [arXiv:2512.01739v2 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.01739v2) (2025). This unconditionally proves the irrationality of $\sum_{n\ge1}\omega(n)/2^n$ and explains why the method does not presently transfer to growing numerators such as $p_n$.
- T. F. Bloom, “Erdős Problem #69,” <https://www.erdosproblems.com/69>, accessed 2026-07-16. This records the prime-factor-series problem solved conditionally by Pratt and unconditionally by Tao and Teräväinen.

### 2. Prime-tuples and short-interval statistics

- P. X. Gallagher, “On the distribution of primes in short intervals,” *Mathematika* 23 (1976), no. 1, 4–9, [DOI 10.1112/S0025579300016442](https://doi.org/10.1112/S0025579300016442). This derives Poisson statistics in logarithmic intervals from uniform fixed-dimensional Hardy–Littlewood prime-tuple conjectures.
- V. Kuperberg, “Sums of singular series with large sets and the tail of the distribution of primes,” *The Quarterly Journal of Mathematics* 74 (2023), no. 4, 1457–1479, [DOI 10.1093/qmath/haad030](https://doi.org/10.1093/qmath/haad030); [arXiv:2210.09775v2 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2210.09775v2). This treats large-set singular-series averages and prime-count tails beyond Gallagher’s fixed-dimension setting.
- J. Pintz, “On the singular series in the prime $k$-tuple conjecture,” [arXiv:1004.1084v1 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1004.1084v1) (2010). This supplies a proof of Gallagher’s singular-series average with uniformity in the tuple dimension.
- H. L. Montgomery and K. Soundararajan, “Primes in Short Intervals,” *Communications in Mathematical Physics* 252 (2004), no. 1–3, 589–617, [DOI 10.1007/s00220-004-1222-4](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00220-004-1222-4); [arXiv:math/0409258v1 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/0409258v1). This gives the refined normal-model prediction for prime counts in mesoscopic short intervals.
- J. Maynard, “Dense clusters of primes in subsets,” *Compositio Mathematica* 152 (2016), no. 7, 1517–1554; [arXiv:1405.2593v2 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1405.2593v2). This supplies dense clusters of primes, but does not prove three consecutive primes in arithmetic progression.
- J. Maynard, “Small gaps between primes,” *Annals of Mathematics* (2) 181 (2015), no. 1, 383–413, [DOI 10.4007/annals.2015.181.1.7](https://doi.org/10.4007/annals.2015.181.1.7); [arXiv:1311.4600v3 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1311.4600v3). This introduces the multidimensional sieve refinement underlying bounded gaps and dense prime clusters.
- A. Granville and A. Lumley, “Primes in short intervals: Heuristics and calculations,” *Experimental Mathematics* 32 (2023), no. 2, 378–404, [DOI 10.1080/10586458.2021.1927256](https://doi.org/10.1080/10586458.2021.1927256); [arXiv:2009.05000v3 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2009.05000v3). This formulates and numerically tests conjectures for extreme prime counts in intervals shorter than the quadratic-logarithmic scale.

### 3. Prime-gap structure and consecutive-prime patterns

- H. Cramér, “On the order of magnitude of the difference between consecutive prime numbers,” *Acta Arithmetica* 2 (1936), 23–46, [DOI 10.4064/aa-2-1-23-46](https://doi.org/10.4064/aa-2-1-23-46). This is the original source of the conjectural quadratic-logarithmic upper scale for prime gaps.
- A. Granville, “Harald Cramér and the distribution of prime numbers,” *Scandinavian Actuarial Journal* 1995, no. 1, 12–28. This explains the small-prime correction to Cramér’s model and the associated constant $2e^{-\gamma}$.
- K. Ford, B. Green, S. Konyagin, J. Maynard, and T. Tao, “Long gaps between primes,” *Journal of the American Mathematical Society* 31 (2018), no. 1, 65–105, [DOI 10.1090/jams/876](https://doi.org/10.1090/jams/876); [arXiv:1412.5029v3 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1412.5029v3). This is the verified large-prime-gap reference.
- W. D. Banks, T. Freiberg, and C. L. Turnage-Butterbaugh, “Consecutive primes in tuples,” *Acta Arithmetica* 167 (2015), no. 3, 261–266; [arXiv:1311.7003v3 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1311.7003v3). This proves consecutive-prime results for constant congruence patterns, not equal consecutive gaps.
- D. K. L. Shiu, “Strings of congruent primes,” *Journal of the London Mathematical Society* (2) 61 (2000), no. 2, 359–373. This proves arbitrarily long strings of consecutive primes in one residue class, not equal consecutive gaps.
- K. Ford, J. Maynard, and T. Tao, “Chains of large gaps between primes,” in *Irregularities in the Distribution of Prime Numbers*, Springer, 2018, 1–21; [arXiv:1511.04468v1 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1511.04468v1). This establishes lower bounds for fixed-length chains of consecutive large prime gaps.
- J. Pintz, “Polignac Numbers, Conjectures of Erdős on Gaps between Primes, Arithmetic Progressions in Primes, and the Bounded Gap Conjecture,” [arXiv:1305.6289v1 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1305.6289v1) (2013). This develops consequences of bounded prime gaps for Polignac numbers and arithmetic progressions.
- A. Hildebrand and H. Maier, “Gaps between prime numbers,” *Proceedings of the American Mathematical Society* 104 (1988), no. 1, 1–9, [DOI 10.1090/S0002-9939-1988-0958032-5](https://doi.org/10.1090/S0002-9939-1988-0958032-5); [verified local PDF](../dossier/S0002-9939-1988-0958032-5.pdf). This proves positive-measure results for limit points of normalized tuples of consecutive prime gaps.
- W. D. Banks, T. Freiberg, and J. Maynard, “On limit points of the sequence of normalized prime gaps,” *Proceedings of the London Mathematical Society* (3) 113 (2016), no. 4, 515–539, [DOI 10.1112/plms/pdw036](https://doi.org/10.1112/plms/pdw036); [arXiv:1404.5094v2 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1404.5094v2). This gives a positive-measure result for limit points of normalized consecutive-prime gaps.
- J. Pintz, “A note on the distribution of normalized prime gaps,” [arXiv:1510.04577v1 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1510.04577v1) (2015). This improves the measure lower bound for the set of normalized-prime-gap limit points.
- J. Merikoski, “Limit points of normalized prime gaps,” *Journal of the London Mathematical Society* (2) 102 (2020), no. 1, 99–124, [DOI 10.1112/jlms.12314](https://doi.org/10.1112/jlms.12314); [arXiv:1811.03008v3 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1811.03008v3). This improves the known measure lower bound for normalized-prime-gap limit points and proves bounded gaps within that limit-point set.
- R. Baker and T. Freiberg, “Limit points and long gaps between primes,” *The Quarterly Journal of Mathematics* 67 (2016), no. 2, 233–260, [DOI 10.1093/qmath/haw003](https://doi.org/10.1093/qmath/haw003); [arXiv:1510.08054v1 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1510.08054v1). This studies limit points of prime gaps normalized at the Erdős–Rankin scale and chains of normalized gaps.
- J. Pintz, “On the distribution of gaps between consecutive primes,” [arXiv:1407.2213v2 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1407.2213v2) (2014). This extends normalized-prime-gap limit-point results to broad classes of normalizing functions.
- H. Maier, “Primes in short intervals,” *Michigan Mathematical Journal* 32 (1985), 221–225, [DOI 10.1307/mmj/1029003189](https://doi.org/10.1307/mmj/1029003189); [verified local PDF](../dossier/Maier-Primes-in-short-intervals.pdf). This is the original source of Maier’s matrix method and the resulting irregularities in short-interval prime counts.
- R. Visser, “Large Gaps Between Primes,” Part III essay, University of Cambridge, 2020; [verified PDF](https://warwick.ac.uk/fac/sci/maths/people/staff/visser/large_gaps_between_primes.pdf). This is a survey of the history, heuristics, computations, and hypergraph-covering method for large prime gaps.

### 4. Consecutive multiplicative functions and prime-factor statistics

- C. F. Lau, “On the Number of Prime Factors of Consecutive Integers,” [arXiv:2604.15042v2 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.15042v2) (2026). This proves infinitely many shifts with the uniform bound $\omega(n+k)\ll\log k$ for all $k\ge2$.
- D. Charamaras and F. K. Richter, “Asymptotic independence of $\Omega(n)$ and $\Omega(n+1)$ along logarithmic averages,” [arXiv:2412.17583v2 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2412.17583v2) (2025). This proves a quantitative logarithmically averaged independence theorem for the total number of prime factors of consecutive integers.
- C. Pilatte, “Improved bounds for the two-point logarithmic Chowla conjecture,” [arXiv:2310.19357v2 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2310.19357v2) (2025). This gives a power saving for the logarithmically averaged two-point Liouville correlation.
- A. P. Mangerel, “On Equal Consecutive Values of Multiplicative Functions,” *Discrete Analysis* 2024:12, DOI 10.19086/da.125450 (**doi.org currently returns HTTP 404; registry correction required**); [journal page](https://discreteanalysisjournal.com/article/125450-on-equal-consecutive-values-of-multiplicative-functions); [author homepage](https://www.durham.ac.uk/staff/alexander-mangerel/); [arXiv:2306.09929v4 PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2306.09929v4). This gives logarithmic-density-zero criteria for equal nonzero values of multiplicative functions at fixed shifts.

### 5. Classical analytic estimates and general references

- J. B. Rosser and L. Schoenfeld, “Approximate formulas for some functions of prime numbers,” *Illinois Journal of Mathematics* 6 (1962), no. 1, 64–94, [DOI 10.1215/ijm/1255631807](https://doi.org/10.1215/ijm/1255631807). This is the verified source for explicit Mertens- and Chebyshev-type estimates.
- F. Mertens, “Über einige asymptotische Gesetze der Zahlentheorie,” *Journal für die reine und angewandte Mathematik* 77 (1874), 289–338. This is the corrected source for Mertens product estimates; volume 78, pages 46–62 is a different paper.
- G. H. Hardy and E. M. Wright, revised by D. R. Heath-Brown and J. H. Silverman, *An Introduction to the Theory of Numbers*, 6th ed., Oxford University Press, 2008, ISBN 978-0-19-921986-5. Chapter I, p. 12, Theorem 8 states $p_n\sim n\log n$.
